# Application Feedback Loop

This file stores lessons learned from previous applications, recruiter feedback, interview outcomes, and self-reflection during the application process.

The AI must read this file before generating new applications or evaluations to continuously improve its framing, answers, and strategies.

## Self-Improvement Rules
1. **Always read this file** before evaluating an offer or drafting an application.
2. **Apply these lessons** directly to the current task.
3. **Self-reflect:** At the end of every application or evaluation, ask yourself if a new trend was spotted, a better way to frame a gap was found, or a new objection was encountered. If so, append a short, actionable lesson to this file.

## Lessons Learned

*   **May 2026 (Intercom Feedback):** Prioritize "Agentic UX" and "B2B SaaS Complexity" over generic UX/UI design terms. Always explicitly use the terms "AI-Native" and "High-Complexity B2B" when the JD mentions AI.
*   **May 2026 (CV Framing):** For Senior UX/UI or Product Design roles, ensure the CV and summaries heavily emphasize Design Thinking strategy, Cross-functional Leadership, Stakeholder Workshops, and business impact/metrics, rather than solely focusing on technical AI features. The balance must shift from execution to impact.
- Re-framing the portfolio link to specifically highlight enterprise workflow case studies (like the KC Overseas Figma project) strongly matches the requirements of highly regulated financial/enterprise roles.
*   **May 2026 (Strategic Framing):** Framing the KC Overseas project as an 'Enterprise Workflow' successfully bridged the gap for a highly regulated financial services role, proving that complex B2B experience can substitute for direct domain knowledge.
